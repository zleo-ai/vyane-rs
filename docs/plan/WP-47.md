# WP-47 — Bounded AgentRun execution driver

Status: delivered

Integrated into the current clean public baseline.

## Purpose

Add the first executable, owner-bound consumer of newly due AgentRuns without
prematurely claiming a resident worker runtime, concrete controller integration,
or session-aware resume. The seam must preserve durable fencing across the
claim-to-effect boundary and leave every uncertain controller for recovery.

## Delivered contract

`vyane-service::AgentRunExecutionDriver` is a fixed-owner, non-`Clone`,
consuming one-shot library driver. One invocation claims at most 64 runs and
polls at most 16 concurrently. Its operation lease is capped at 300 seconds;
heartbeat cadence is bounded from 100 milliseconds through 60 seconds and must
remain strictly below that lease.

The driver establishes its local monotonic base before claim. The complete
blocking claim operation is admitted on Tokio's blocking pool before any item
work starts. For each valid claim it:

1. generates a prospective controller handle containing independent,
   domain-separated 256-bit identifier and 256-bit fingerprint material;
2. durably starts the exact controller;
3. issues an `ActiveExecutionPermit` from the resulting running receipt;
4. completes a pre-effect heartbeat; and
5. only then performs the executor's first poll.

The trusted executor contract must atomically revalidate that permit at every
external-effect linearization point. Receiving the permit or passing the first
heartbeat is not blanket authority for later effects. Custom `AgentStore`
implementations must close every transition with exact owner, run, generation,
token, lease, controller, state, and returned-receipt validation; the driver
does not infer correctness from a trait call merely returning success.

The current receipt has a single writer inside an item future. Heartbeats and
terminal settlement serialize through it rather than racing independent
copies. Only an executor outcome of `Quiesced` is proof that all effects have
stopped and authorizes the driver to initiate a closed terminal settlement.
Before that proof, cancellation, timeout, panic, driver drop, `Unknown`, or
heartbeat failure authorizes no new settlement and may leave the run
nonterminal as `Starting` or `Running` for WP-45 exact-identity recovery. Once
a `Quiesced` settlement call has started on the blocking pool it is fully
awaited by the live driver; later cancellation cannot interrupt it, and dropping
the waiter cannot stop the blocking store call. A custom store may also
mutate-then-error, so a reported settlement failure is uncertain rather than
proof that no terminal mutation occurred.

## Verification

Hermetic tests cover option and metadata rejection, cancellation before claim,
blocking-pool claim admission, custom-store transition validation, prospective
handle uniqueness and shape, claim/start/permit/heartbeat/effect ordering,
heartbeat renewal with a single current receipt, bounded concurrency,
quiesced-only settlement admission, nonterminal `Starting`/`Running` handoff,
and uncertain timeout/cancellation/panic/drop/store-error paths. The delivery gate is full formatting, strict
Clippy, workspace tests, secret scanning, and independent review.

## Non-goals and remaining parity work

WP-47 does not provide a concrete executor or controller-recovery adapter. It
does not add a resident loop, production service/CLI/daemon assembly, message
correlation or handback, a session-aware authority composition, live resume,
or automatic replay. The generated controller identity is a prerequisite for
exact recovery, not proof that any external runtime already implements that
contract. Consequently WP-47 advances AgentRun execution and supervision
foundations without changing whole-system parity status.
