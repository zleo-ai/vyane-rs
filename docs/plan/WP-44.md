# WP-44 — Explicit resident broker supervisor

## Goal

Compose the existing bounded message broker and EventLog projectors into one
explicit resident library driver without creating a second queue, hidden task
owner or production daemon integration.

Integrated into the current clean public baseline.

## Explicit construction and admission

`vyane-broker::ResidentBrokerSupervisor` is deliberately non-`Clone` and owns:

- one owner-bound `MessageBroker`;
- one `MessageEventProjector` over the exact same `Arc` message store;
- one owner-matched `AgentEventProjector`;
- zero or more `DeliveryLane` values; and
- one validated `SupervisorOptions` value.

Construction rejects mismatched owner scopes or message-store identity,
duplicate lane ids, overlapping mailbox sets, invalid projector identities and
delivery concurrency above the configured aggregate ceiling. Each lane freezes
its claim query, lease request, replay-safe adapter and pump options before any
claim can run. Lane admission validates those values, including the adapter's
stable non-secret identity/replay contract and the lease/adapter-timeout
relationship.

## Caller-owned resident run

`run(self, CancellationToken)` consumes the supervisor and returns one future.
That future directly polls independent delivery-lane, maintenance, message-
projection and AgentEvent-projection loops concurrently. It does not call
`tokio::spawn`, create an internal channel or runtime, read global environment
configuration, or maintain another pending/in-flight queue. The embedding
caller owns the Tokio runtime, the future and its cancellation/drain policy.

The loops remain independent:

- one delivery-lane error, degraded batch or panic backs off only that lane;
- maintenance continues even when there are no delivery lanes;
- message and AgentEvent projection make progress independently of delivery and
  of each other; and
- every error or panic payload is discarded rather than copied into durable
  state or the exit report.

Successful busy cycles yield to the runtime. Empty delivery/projector cycles
sleep for the idle interval, maintenance sleeps for its own interval, and a
full maintenance or projector page yields before the next bounded page.
Failures use deterministic capped exponential backoff and successful
non-degraded work resets the lane's backoff.

## Bounds and cancellation

The default configuration uses a 250 ms idle interval, a 30 s maintenance
interval, 250 ms to 30 s error backoff, a maintenance batch of 128, a projection
batch of 16 and aggregate delivery concurrency of 32. Validation enforces:

- maintenance and projection batches in `1..=128`;
- configured aggregate delivery ceiling in `1..=256`; the effective sum may
  be zero when no delivery lanes are configured and must not exceed that
  ceiling;
- each scheduling/backoff duration greater than zero and at most 24 hours; and
- initial backoff no greater than maximum backoff.

Cancellation is observed only between cycles. A loop that observes the token
starts no new cycle; if cancellation arrives during an already-started bounded
one-shot operation, that operation and any delivery settlement are awaited
before `SupervisorExit` is returned. A caller may wrap the complete future in a
wider timeout, but dropping it forfeits graceful drain because an already-
running blocking store call can outlive its async waiter.

`SupervisorExit` contains only lane ids and saturating body-free counters for
cycles, success, failure, panic, work and degraded items. It does not expose
message bodies, delivery destinations, receipts, lease tokens, adapter error
text or panic payloads. `DeliveryLane::Debug` likewise omits mailbox identity
and message data.

## Acceptance

- Constructor tests reject invalid pre-claim input, mismatched owners,
  duplicate mailbox ownership and invalid supervisor bounds, and statically
  prove that lanes and the supervisor are not `Clone`.
- Paused-time tests prove that a pre-cancelled run starts zero cycles and claims
  nothing, while cancellation during an adapter call waits for the current
  delivery to settle.
- Deterministic paused-time tests prove capped exponential backoff after adapter
  panics.
- A broken lane and failing message projector do not stop a healthy lane or the
  AgentEvent projector.
- Maintenance expires due messages even with zero delivery lanes.
- Repository format, Clippy-with-warnings-denied, workspace tests, independent
  review and staged secret scanning remain merge requirements.

## Integration state and non-goals

This is the first narrow resident library driver described by
[ADR 0004](../adr/0004-modular-supervisor-host.md), not the future composed host.
No `vyane-service` factory or operation, CLI command, workflow daemon or other
production assembly constructs it. Ordinary dispatch remains unchanged.

WP-44 does not provide:

- AgentRun execution, worker-health scanning, controller recovery or the future
  session-aware authority left open by [WP-42](WP-42.md);
- controller/message correlation or handback glue;
- authenticated public service/CLI message or execution APIs;
- A2A or Channels adapters; or
- live pause/resume, automatic restart replay or implicit recovery.

Those consumers must be composed explicitly in later work without weakening
the one-store truth, owner binding, fencing and bounded-drain contracts.
