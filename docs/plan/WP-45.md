# WP-45 — Bounded one-shot AgentRun stale-controller recovery

## Goal

Consume the durable AgentRun store's existing two-stage recovery protocol
through one explicit fixed-owner service seam, without adding a resident loop,
new executor, raw-store escape hatch or production runtime integration.

Integrated into the current clean public baseline.

## Explicit construction and authority boundary

`vyane-service::AgentRunRecoveryDriver` is deliberately non-`Clone`.
Construction freezes:

- one canonical owner;
- one injected, encapsulated `Arc<dyn AgentStore>`;
- one stable reconciler identity;
- one validated `AgentRecoveryOptions` value; and
- zero or more trusted `AgentControllerAdapter` registrations, with at most one
  stable non-secret name per exact `ControllerKind`.

Construction performs no store operation, so invalid owner, reconciler,
options or adapter metadata fails before a durable claim. The raw store and
`RecoveryTicket` are never exposed. An adapter receives only the exact
body-free `ControllerRef` and a monotonic deadline; it receives no owner,
operation id, recovery token, store handle or settlement authority.

The adapter contract is intentionally stronger than a process-name probe.
Before every external control effect it must revalidate the complete exact
identity, including the fingerprint when present. If the identity cannot
exclude controller reuse, the adapter must return `Unavailable` without an
effect. `Gone` is valid only when that exact controller is already absent, or
after the adapter synchronously stops it and observes exit. Stop/observe work
must remain safe to repeat or reconcile after timeout, caller drop or durable
settlement failure. Adapters must not detach unowned control work, and panic
payloads must not contain controller data, credentials or bodies because a
process panic hook runs before the driver catches the unwind.

## One bounded consuming pass

`recover_once(self, CancellationToken)` consumes the driver. It does not create
a resident task, internal channel, runtime, second queue or per-item spawned
task. The caller owns the Tokio runtime and decides whether to construct a
later pass.

The default pass uses a batch of 32, concurrency of 8, a 20-second adapter
timeout, a 5-second settlement margin and a 30-second operation lease. Hard
validation bounds are:

- recovery batch `1..=64`;
- adapter concurrency `1..=16` and no greater than the batch;
- adapter timeout greater than zero and at most 60 seconds;
- settlement margin greater than zero and at most 60 seconds; and
- operation lease `1..=300` seconds, strictly longer than adapter timeout plus
  settlement margin.

Before entering `spawn_blocking(claim_recovery_due)`, the driver derives a
caller-local monotonic pass deadline from the configured operation lease. Claim
latency is therefore deducted. Every adapter call uses one `call_started`
instant for both its remaining-window check and its checked deadline, and the
driver polls it with `timeout_at` on that same deadline. This keeps the adapter
deadline strictly before the pass deadline even at the minimum margin. Durable
ticket wall-clock expiry remains solely a store-side confirmation fence; an
offset custom-store clock cannot extend the driver's adapter-admission window.

The claimed batch is validated in full before any adapter is polled. A store
result above the requested limit, a malformed ticket, wrong reconciler or
duplicate operation/run fails closed without an adapter call. Because the
claim may already be durable, those operations remain fenced until their
leases expire and a later pass may reclaim them.

## Proof and settlement

A controllerless ticket can proceed directly to confirmation. For a ticket
with a controller, only `ControllerRecoveryObservation::Gone` can call
`confirm_controller_gone`; `StillPresent`, `Unavailable`, missing or invalid
adapter, insufficient local window, cancellation, timeout and panic never
authorize settlement. Each confirmation also runs on Tokio's blocking pool and
uses the unchanged internal recovery ticket, so the store remains the final
revision/generation/token/expiry authority.

`AgentRecoveryReport`, item status, error and driver/context `Debug` output are
body-free. They omit owner, run/worker/controller identity, operation id,
ticket/token, credentials, raw store errors and raw panic payloads. The report
contains only a claimed count, completion-ordered closed statuses and whether
cancellation was already visible before claim.

## Cancellation, timeout and drop

Cancellation observed before claim returns a zero-claim report and performs no
store mutation. Once claim starts, already-running blocking claim or settlement
work is awaited; cancellation suppresses buffered adapter calls that have not
started, while an in-flight adapter is polled only until its shared monotonic
deadline. An affirmative result may still be settled after cancellation
because the exact controller-gone proof is already available.

Dropping the outer future is not graceful. Tokio cannot abort a running
`spawn_blocking` store call, a custom store may block indefinitely, settlement
may complete after the async waiter disappears, and an adapter's
platform-specific non-abortable blocking work may continue after its future is
timed out or dropped. Such adapter work must be independently bounded,
exact-identity-safe and retry-safe. A future timeout bounds polling; it is not
proof that an external effect stopped.

## Acceptance

- Constructor tests reject invalid owner/reconciler/options, invalid or
  duplicate adapter registration and hard-bound violations before claim.
- Owner-isolation tests recover one owner without claiming another owner's
  stale run.
- Controllerless and exact `Gone` cases settle; still-present, unavailable,
  timeout, panic and missing-adapter cases do not.
- Cancellation before claim is non-mutating; cancellation after claim prevents
  a buffered second adapter call.
- Concurrency never exceeds the frozen cap.
- A far-future custom store clock plus a concurrent second reconciler proves
  that the old pass cannot start another adapter after its caller-local safe
  window.
- A one-nanosecond margin regression proves the adapter deadline remains
  strictly earlier than the pass deadline.
- Static assertions keep the driver non-`Clone` and non-serializable; report,
  error and `Debug` checks remain redacted.
- Repository format, Clippy-with-warnings-denied, workspace tests, independent
  review and staged secret scanning remain merge requirements.

## Integration state and non-goals

WP-45 adds no concrete `AgentControllerAdapter`. It is not:

- a resident worker-health or AgentRun recovery loop;
- an AgentRun claim/start/heartbeat/execute supervisor or new executor;
- session-aware authority assembly, native resume or automatic new-run replay;
- production factory, service operation, CLI, workflow-daemon or resident-host
  assembly;
- controller/message correlation or handback glue;
- A2A or Channels integration; or
- live pause/resume or automatic daemon-restart replay.

A later modular host may repeatedly construct this one-shot seam behind a
resident AgentRun execution supervisor, but only with a concrete
exact-identity-safe controller adapter, explicit owner context, bounded retry
and the separate session/domain authority required for session-aware resume.
See [ADR 0004](../adr/0004-modular-supervisor-host.md).
